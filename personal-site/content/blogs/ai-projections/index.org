#+title: What if AI doesn't grow?
#+date: [2026-06-19 Fri] 

The problem with projections about the growth of AI is that they are just
projections. By their nature, projections are right when their assumptions about
reality are right. We don't linearly project the growth of toddlers and assume
they will become 20 feet tall (and be very good in the NBA) because of our prior
knowledge about what human growth dynamics are like. We don't have comparable
knowledge about the growth dynamics of a new technology like LLMs. In hindsight
we will see that some projections were right and some were wrong. "Pessimists
sound smart. Optimists make money." I think optimism is great, but let's not get
it confused with a law of nature.
